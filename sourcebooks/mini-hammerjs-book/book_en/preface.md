# Preface: Unveiling the Mysteries of Gesture Interaction with Your Own Hands

In today's world of ubiquitous smart devices, touch and gesture interactions have become our instinct for communicating with the digital world. Whether it's a simple tap, a fluid drag, or a two-finger zoom, behind these smooth and natural interactions lies the support of powerful gesture recognition technology.

Have you ever wondered how these seemingly magical gestures are accurately captured and recognized? Have you ever dreamed of building your own gesture library to give your applications a more dynamic and interactive experience?

If your answer is yes, then this book is written for you.

This book will use the famous gesture library `Hammer.js` as a blueprint to take you on an exciting journey. We will not get lost in the vast and complex source code of `Hammer.js`. Instead, we will take a different path, focusing on its core design philosophy and implementation principles. Together, we will build a fully functional mini-version of `Hammer.js`—`mini-hammerjs`—from scratch, step by step.

In this book, you will learn:

*   **Event-Driven Architecture**: Understand how `Hammer.js` decouples input events from gesture recognition.
*   **The State Machine Model of Gesture Recognizers**: Master the complete lifecycle of a gesture, from "possible" to "recognized".
*   **Implementation Principles of Core Gestures**: Dive deep into the logic and algorithmic details of core gestures like `Tap`, `Pan`, and `Press`.
*   **The Building Process from Zero to One**: Write code with your own hands, transforming theoretical knowledge into a tangible `mini-hammerjs` library.

The goal of this book is not to train a user of `Hammer.js`, but to create a creator of gesture libraries. We have deliberately ignored engineering details such as performance optimization and cross-browser compatibility, solely to allow you to focus distraction-free on implementing the core functionality.

After reading this book, you will not only thoroughly understand the essence of `Hammer.js` but also possess the ability to design and implement a gesture library independently.

Now, let's unveil the mysteries of gesture interaction together and embark on this exciting journey of creation!
